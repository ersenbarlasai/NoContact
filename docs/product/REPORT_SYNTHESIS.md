# Report Synthesis

# Strongest Shared Findings
- The best product hook is not "breakup advice"; it is "open this before you message them."
- SOS / emergency mode is the strongest retention and differentiation point.
- AI message analysis is the killer feature because it helps users pause before reacting.
- Mood tracking plus streaks makes progress visible and helps users believe recovery is happening.
- A structured healing journey gives the app depth beyond a simple counter.
- Unsent letters are therapeutically useful but require strong privacy handling.
- SEO and education content are important for acquisition, but the mobile app must deliver immediate relief first.

# Main Gaps To Close
- Pricing must be transparent from the beginning.
- AI boundaries must be explicit and repeated in sensitive moments.
- Data security must be a visible product feature, not hidden legal text.
- Current category products often under-serve men and culturally diverse users.
- Community is missing in many competitors, but must be moderated and constrained.
- Existing products do not appear to track SOS outcomes deeply enough.
- Accessibility, performance, structured content, and localization need first-class ownership.

# MVP Priority
1. Personal onboarding and recovery plan.
2. Home screen with day counter, mood, recommended action, and persistent SOS.
3. SOS flow with breathing, rule reminders, urge timer, and after-action reflection.
4. AI message analysis with clear safety disclaimers.
5. Mood journal and weekly insights.
6. Unsent letters vault.
7. Guided exercises.
8. Subscription gates and transparent plan comparison.

# Later Roadmap
- Voice SOS coach.
- Anonymous buddy or group challenge.
- Professional/therapist referral directory.
- Wearable reminders.
- PWA and web writing experience.
- Clinician dashboard.
- Content/SEO site with localized breakup guides.

# First Screen Direction
The first screen should be an actual dashboard, not a marketing page:
- Big no-contact day count.
- Clear status line: "Bugun temas etmeden devam ediyorsun."
- One large SOS action.
- One tiny mood check-in.
- One next step: breathing, journal, message analysis, or exercise.
