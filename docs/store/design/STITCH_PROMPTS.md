# Google Stitch Prompts - NoContact / Nosto

Paste these prompts directly into Google Stitch (or any AI design tool) to generate base assets for the app store.

## 1. App Store Screenshot Set (iOS)
> "Create a set of 7 App Store screenshots for an iOS app called 'Nosto'. The app helps people regulate their emotions during a 'no contact' phase after a breakup. Use a minimal, mobile-first design system with Sage Green, Cream, and Terracotta colors. Avoid dating app clichés (no broken hearts) and medical app clichés (no crosses). Backgrounds should be a soft Cream. At the top of each image, place a dark bold Turkish headline and a smaller caption. Place a flat, modern iPhone 14 Pro frame below the text. The screens should show: 1. A clean dashboard with a day counter. 2. A calm SOS breathing interface. 3. A locked 'letters vault' list. 4. A simple mood journal slider. 5. A quiet progress insight screen. 6. A gentle notification settings screen. 7. A Face ID privacy lock prompt."

## 2. Play Store Screenshot Set (Android)
> "Create a set of 7 Google Play Store screenshots for an Android app called 'Nosto'. The app is a privacy-first emotional regulation journal. Use a minimal design system with Sage Green, Cream, and Terracotta colors. Avoid dating app clichés and medical app clichés. Backgrounds should be a soft Sage Green. At the top of each image, place a dark bold Turkish headline and a smaller caption. Place a flat, modern Android phone frame below the text. The screens should show: 1. A clean dashboard with a day counter. 2. A calm SOS breathing interface. 3. A locked 'letters vault' list. 4. A simple mood journal slider. 5. A quiet progress insight screen. 6. A gentle notification settings screen. 7. A biometric fingerprint privacy lock prompt."

## 3. App Icon Exploration
> "Design an iOS app icon for a self-care and emotional regulation app called 'Nosto'. The visual style must be extremely calm, mature, and grounding. Use a palette of Sage Green, Cream, and a touch of Terracotta. DO NOT use broken hearts, chat bubbles, crosses, or dating app gradients. The design should be abstract and quiet. Ideas: A smooth, asymmetrical organic pebble shape, a subtle pause symbol rendered softly, or a minimal calming horizon line. It should look trustworthy and private."

## 4. Home Dashboard Visual Refinement
> "Design a mobile app home screen for a self-regulation app called 'Nosto'. The UI must be highly minimal and use a Sage Green, Cream, and Terracotta color palette. At the top, show a simple counter: '14 Gündür İletişimde Değilsin'. Below it, a calm, non-gamified greeting. Show two large, soft-edged buttons: 'SOS' and 'Duygu Günlüğü'. The design must feel spacious, quiet, and safe, avoiding any loud gamification elements."

## 5. SOS Screen Visual Refinement
> "Design a mobile app SOS screen meant to calm panic or anxiety. The background should be a deeply calming Sage Green or Cream. In the center, a soft, abstract breathing circle or a minimal text input box labeled 'Buraya Yaz ve Sil' (Write and Delete). The typography should be Sans-serif, mature, and reassuring. No medical alerts, no red emergency colors. Use Terracotta only as a gentle accent."

## 6. Privacy / Encrypted Local Storage Visual
> "Design a mobile app screen showing a 'Letters Vault' (Mektup Kasası). The UI should look like a simple, elegant list of saved notes, but with a strong emphasis on privacy. Include a subtle, friendly 'locked' or 'encrypted' badge. The text should read 'Buraya yazdıkların bu cihazda şifreli saklanır' (What you write here is encrypted on this device). Use Sage Green and Cream. The aesthetic must be trustworthy and private, not alarming."

## 7. Quiet Growth / Insights Visual
> "Design a mobile app progress screen called 'Sessiz Gelişim' (Quiet Growth). It tracks emotional stability over time. DO NOT use aggressive streak counters, fire emojis, or loud graphs. Use soft, muted bar charts or simple text summaries showing 'Yönetilen İstek' (Managed Urges) and 'Tamamlanan Adımlar' (Completed Steps). Use Cream background with Sage Green data visualizations. The feeling should be reflective and patient."
