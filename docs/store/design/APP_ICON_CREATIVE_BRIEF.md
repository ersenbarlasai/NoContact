# App Icon Creative Brief - NoContact / Still

## 1. Brand Objective
The app icon is the first touchpoint for a user who is likely feeling overwhelmed, anxious, or heartbroken. The icon must communicate safety, stillness, and privacy. It should NOT broadcast to anyone looking at their phone screen that they are going through a breakup or using a mental health crisis app.

## 2. Color Palette
- **Primary Base:** Cream (`#FAF9F6`) or Sage Green (`#849A89`)
- **Accent Symbol:** Terracotta (`#C27E6A`) or deep Sage.
- **Contrast:** Avoid pure white and pure black.

## 3. Shape Language
- **Soft and Organic:** Use rounded corners, asymmetrical natural shapes (like stones or leaves).
- **Grounded:** Visual weight should feel anchored, not floating away.
- **Minimalist:** Remove all unnecessary details. The icon must read clearly at 16x16 pixels.

## 4. Concept Directions
### Concept A: The Pebble (Recommended)
A single, smooth, organically shaped pebble.
- **Why:** Pebbles represent grounding, nature, and the passage of time smoothing out rough edges.
- **Execution:** Cream background, Sage Green pebble, slight soft shadow.

### Concept B: The Soft Pause
A non-traditional "pause" icon.
- **Why:** The app is about pausing before reacting (No Contact).
- **Execution:** Two vertical, pill-shaped lines, but slightly irregular or organic, in Terracotta on a Sage background.

### Concept C: The Horizon
A simple, calm horizon line.
- **Why:** Represents looking forward, a new day, and vast calm space.
- **Execution:** Bottom half muted Terracotta, top half Cream, separated by a soft line.

## 5. Rejected Concepts (DO NOT USE)
- **Broken Hearts:** Reinforces trauma, looks like a cliché dating app feature.
- **Plasters / Band-Aids:** Looks like a medical or first-aid app.
- **Chat Bubbles with a Slash:** Too aggressive, focuses on the "No" rather than the "Still".
- **Padlocks:** Looks like a password manager or generic security app.

## 6. App Store Visibility Notes
- The icon must stand out against both Light Mode and Dark Mode wallpapers. A distinct border or slight inner shadow may be needed if using a Cream background.
- It must not blend in with generic utility apps (avoid generic blue/gray).

## 7. Accessibility Considerations
- Ensure adequate contrast between the background and the central symbol.
- Do not rely solely on thin lines; the central motif must have enough thickness to be legible for users with low vision.
